The hidden story about Zanzibar

Zanzibar Island also known as Zanzibar archipelago, is the part of Tanzania country in East Africa Coast. Zanzibar is a semi-autonomous state within the United Republic of Tanzania, why because Zanzibar united with Tanganyika on 26 April 1964 to form Tanzania.

Historically, Zanzibar is not just a single island, is the combination of two large islands UNGUJA and PEMBA. UNGUJA is the main island in Zanzibar and considered most of the biggest island in Zanzibar archipelago and in addition, it is where the capital city of Zanzibar found (The Stone Town). Apart of these two biggest island in Zanzibar, that is UNGUJA and PEMBA but also there are small islands more than 70 found within the Zanzibar archipelago. Many of these small islands not habited by people but mostly used for recreations, enjoyment and other tourism purpose. Example of such small island include changuu island famous known as Prison Island, Mnemba Island, Chumbe Island, Tumbatu Island and Bawe Island.


Why Zanzibar city/town called stone town

To cut the story short, Zanzibar city/ town also known as ‘’The Stone Town’’ why because most of the buildings that are found in Zanzibar town were constructed with local stone (reddish coral stone) that were used as the main construction materials for building s and houses at Zanzibar town. Therefore many of the houses and buildings around Zanzibar town were built with local stone by Arab traders and African slavers during the 19th Century.

For this reason, the Zanzibar town/city Nicknamed as The Stone Town. So that to make it clear, keep in mind that Zanzibar city or Zanzibar town is Stone Town.




What makes Zanzibar so special in tourism

Zanzibar settled by people of different colors. It the place that you can see many cultures, includes Swahili, Arabs, Indians, Shiraz, British, Chinese, Italian, Poland, Germany and among others just to mention a few. Due to that Zanzibar is known for its ancient architecture and street food culture. Therefore for that reason Zanzibar Island is the only place in the world that you can find a lot of language spoken by local tour guides like English, France, Portuguese, Russian, Italian, Poland, Serbia, Spanish, Germany, and Chinese and among others.

So saying Zanzibar it is the only destination in world that a visitor can choose guide to his or her own language.

Peoples, religion and culture

Zanzibar inhabited by people of different origins and ethnic mixture of many different backgrounds including Arabs, mainlanders, Shiraz’s and Asians (Indians). In term of religion Zanzibar is the home of Muslim about over 90% Muslim (mainly Sunni but including many minorities as well). There are also different Christians, Hindus and other, smaller religions represented on the island. Due to that, Zanzibar dominated by Muslim culture famous known as Swahili culture in east Africa coast.




Climate

Climate in Zanzibar is warm all year round. Yet, strong sea breezes often cool the heat of summer (November to March). As a result, this time of the year the time of the northeast monsoon, particularly on the north and east coasts. Rains occur in November usually with brief showers. Longer rains occur from March to May in association with the southwest monsoon.
